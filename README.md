# 📱 Social Media Performance Analysis Dashboard
**Tool:** Microsoft Excel | **Dataset:** 450 posts across 4 platforms | **Domain:** Digital Marketing Analytics

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project delivers a full social media analytics solution for a multi-platform brand operating across TikTok, Instagram, Facebook and Twitter/X. The analysis covers 450 posts spanning 12 months and 6 campaigns — uncovering what drives engagement, which platforms deliver the best return on investment and what separates viral content from average posts.

The analysis was built entirely in Microsoft Excel using PivotTables, advanced formulas and data visualisation — progressing from raw data cleaning through to a fully interactive dashboard designed for non-technical stakeholders.

## 📊 Analysis — 11 Business Questions

---

**Q1. Which platform delivers the highest engagement rate?**

| Platform | Engagement Rate (Reach) |
|---|---|
| TikTok | 12.34% |
| Instagram | 8.96% |
| Twitter/X | 5.32% |
| Facebook | 4.39% |

**Insight:** TikTok delivers nearly 3x the engagement rate of Facebook. For brands prioritising reach and interaction, TikTok is the clear priority platform.

---

**Q2. Which content type delivers the highest ROI when production cost is factored in?**

| Content Type | ROI Engagement |
|---|---|
| Video | 9.56 |
| Carousel | 6.79 |
| Image | 6.08 |
| Text | 3.51 |

**Insight:** Video content generates the highest return on production investment at 9.56 — nearly 3x the return of text posts. Budget should be prioritised toward video production.

---

**Q3. Which campaigns generated the most engagement and follower growth?**

| Campaign | Total Engagement | Followers Gained |
|---|---|---|
| Summer Vibes | 269,120 | 2,391 |
| Spring Launch | 227,078 | 2,833 |
| New Year Reset | 139,646 | 1,452 |
| Holiday Magic | 101,768 | 1,188 |

**Insight:** Summer Vibes and Spring Launch are the strongest campaigns by engagement volume. Spring Launch delivers the best follower growth — suggesting seasonal launch campaigns are the most effective for audience building.

---

**Q4. Does higher impressions lead to meaningful engagement?**

Pearson Correlation Coefficient: **0.78**

**Insight:** There is a strong positive correlation between impressions and engagement. However the relationship is not perfect — indicating that content quality and targeting also play a significant role beyond reach alone.

---

**Q5. Are boosted posts worth the investment?**

| Boost Status | Total Engagement | Avg Engagement | CPE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Boosted | 478,212 | 3,542 | £0.144 |
| Organic | 392,761 | 1,247 | £0.124 |

**Insight:** Boosted posts generate 2.8x more average engagement per post. However the cost per engagement is slightly higher at £0.144 vs £0.124 organically. Boosting is worth the investment for high-performing content but should not be applied indiscriminately.

---

**Q6. Does optimal posting time vary by platform?**

| Platform | Best Time |
|---|---|
| TikTok | Afternoon (14.5% engagement rate) |
| Instagram | Evening (10.6% engagement rate) |
| Twitter/X | Afternoon (6.0% engagement rate) |
| Facebook | Afternoon (5.9% engagement rate) |

**Insight:** Optimal posting time varies significantly by platform. TikTok and Facebook peak in the afternoon while Instagram performs best in the evening. A platform-specific scheduling strategy will outperform a one-size-fits-all approach.

---

**Q7. What is the relationship between production quality (cost) and engagement?**

Pearson Correlation Coefficient: **0.15**

**Insight:** Production cost has a very weak positive correlation with engagement. Higher spend does not reliably produce higher engagement — reinforcing that content relevance and platform fit matter more than production budget.

---

**Q8. What is the viral content profile?**

Viral posts share the following characteristics:
- Platform: Predominantly TikTok and Instagram
- Content Type: Video (dominant) and Carousel
- Boost Status: Mix of boosted and organic — virality is not purely pay-to-play
- Engagement: Viral posts average 10,000+ engagements vs 353 for normal posts

**Insight:** Viral content on TikTok is video-first and does not require boosting to achieve outsized reach. Instagram viral posts are predominantly Carousel format.

---

**Q9. Which platform gives the best follower growth per post?**

| Platform | Total Engagement | Total Posts |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 486,301 | 124 |
| Instagram | 238,072 | 129 |
| Twitter/X | 97,622 | 118 |
| Facebook | 48,978 | 79 |

**Insight:** TikTok generates nearly double the engagement of Instagram with a similar number of posts — making it the most efficient platform for audience growth and engagement per post published.

---

**Q10. Does hashtag count affect engagement performance?**

Pearson Correlation Coefficient: **0.10**

Hashtags 7-8 show the highest engagement rates (11.1% and 13.4% respectively).

**Insight:** Hashtag count has a weak positive correlation with engagement. However posts using 7-8 targeted hashtags consistently outperform both lower and higher hashtag counts — suggesting an optimal range rather than a linear relationship.

---

**Q11. What is the cost per engagement across platforms?**

| Platform | Total Engagement | Total Cost | CPE |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 486,301 | £35,277 | £0.073 |
| Instagram | 238,072 | £35,008 | £0.147 |
| Facebook | 48,978 | £15,764 | £0.322 |
| Twitter/X | 97,622 | £31,524 | £0.323 |

**Insight:** TikTok delivers the lowest cost per engagement at £0.073 — more than 4x cheaper than Facebook and Twitter/X. For budget-conscious campaigns TikTok offers the strongest commercial efficiency.

---

## 💡 Overall Business Conclusions

**Platform Strategy:** TikTok dominates on every efficiency metric — engagement rate, follower growth and cost per engagement. Brands should prioritise TikTok as their primary platform while maintaining Instagram for Carousel-driven engagement.

**Content Strategy:** Video content generates the highest ROI at 9.56. Investment in video production delivers measurable returns across TikTok and Instagram.

**Campaigns:** Spring and Summer campaigns outperform across both engagement and follower acquisition. Seasonal campaign timing should be built into the annual content calendar.

**Boosting:** Apply boosting selectively to high-performing organic content rather than all posts. Blanket boosting increases CPE without proportional engagement gains.

**Posting Schedule:** Platform-specific scheduling — afternoon for TikTok, Facebook and Twitter/X; evening for Instagram — will improve organic performance without additional spend.
